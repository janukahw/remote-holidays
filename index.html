How the ranking works

Measured by what you can't get

We re-score every listing — all five providers — on a single 0–100 remoteness scale, then rank them highest first. The further up, the harder it is for the world to reach you.

◬ Off-grid power

No mains electricity — solar, generator, candlelight. The strongest signal that you're properly off the map.

⌁ No WiFi

Nothing to connect to. No streaming, no "just an hour of work", no doomscrolling in bed.

⌖ No / poor signal

Your phone is a brick for calls and data. Many of these are also a long track, boat or tide from the nearest road.

Each place is scored out of 100 on seven things — access, mobile signal, off-grid services, WiFi, how "cut off" it sounds, distance to the nearest shop or station, and how wild the setting is — so a National Trust cottage and an off-grid cabin sit on the same ruler. Open any place to see its breakdown. Where an off-grid, remote place doesn't mention phone signal, we assume it has little or none — so a terse listing doesn't outrank an honest one. Scoring reads each listing's own words, so it reflects how a place is described, not measured GPS isolation — always confirm details on the provider's own site before booking.

An independent, non-commercial guide to the remotest places to stay in the UK — from National Trust, Unplugged, Unyoked, Canopy & Stars, Cool Places and more to come.

Names, photos, listing links and location data belong to their respective providers (National Trust, Unplugged, Unyoked, Canopy & Stars, Cool Places), shown here for reference. Not affiliated with or endorsed by them.

A note on safety. Remoteness scores (0–100) are judged from listing copy and can be wrong or out of date. "No signal" cuts both ways — you can't be reached in an emergency either. Tell someone your plans, carry a map and the means to navigate without a phone, and confirm details with the provider before you travel.
